Empowering entrepreneurs across Africa through digital solutions

Entrepreneurs are a vital part of the inclusive, sustainable growth that is so needed for sub-Saharan Africa, and addressing the growing needs of micro, small and medium-size enterprises (MSMEs) is fundamental to job creation and wealth development..

Our customers on Airtel Money’s safe, simple and convenient mobile payments system can send and receive money, and gain access to other mobile money services, including savings accounts and instant microloans. This enables users to build a credible payment history, which is particularly valuable for MSMEs who come with little financial backing or history, struggle to meet most traditional lenders’ requirements and lack access to working capital.

Through our international money transfer services and strategic partnerships with Mastercard, Ecobank, Western Union, Standard Chartered Bank, MoneyGram and World Remit, among others, Airtel Money customers are more connected to the global economy than ever before.

Our purpose is to transform the lives of people across sub-Saharan Africa

By increasing digital and financial inclusion, we’re transforming lives – connecting people, delivering access to essential services and building better financial futures. We provide connectivity in our 14 markets – it is pivotal in addressing inequality and building better futures for communities across Africa.

In June 2024 we published Airtel Africa’s Sustainability Report 2024, reflecting our commitment to environmental, social and governance (ESG) practices over the financial year 2023/24. This report details how we’re implementing responsible business practices across our markets by focusing on our sustainability performance. It highlights our achievements and plans for future initiatives that will positively impact the environment and communities in which we operate.

In this report we demonstrate how we align our sustainability efforts with standards set by the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) and GSMA’s recommendations for the telecommunications industry. We also adhere to the Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) and the London Stock Exchange (LSE) recommendations in the Annual Report to maintain transparency and accountability.

We recognise Airtel Africa’s responsibility to implement climate change mitigation and environmental protection programmes. In addition, we focus on maintaining high corporate governance standards, the protection of human and labour rights and community development initiatives. The report outlines our progress toward the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals (UN SDGs), highlighting our contributions to sustainable development.
